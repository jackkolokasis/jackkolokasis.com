About

Iacovos G. Kolokasis (nickname Jack) is a master student in Computer Science Department at University of Crete, working with Prof. Angelos Bilas and Prof. Polyvios Pratikakis He offers his collaboration in Computer Architecture and VLSI (CARV) Laboratory of the Institute of Computer Science (ICS) at Foundation of Research and Technology – Hellas (FORTH). Currently, he is working as an intern research student in SAP HANA Vora at SAP SE, in Germany.

His main research interests are in the general area of computer systems with an emphasis on memory and storage systems and high-performance data analytics frameworks. He is particularly interested in emerging memory technologies (e.g., non-volatile memories) into future data centers that execute various applications, e.g., machine learning, big-data analytics and scientific computing.

    Thesis: Locality Optimizations Distributed Graph Analysis Computations
    Supervisor: Polyvios Pratikakis
    My Bachelor’s thesis was based on Graph Analysis and Management on Distributed Systems. The goal of my work was to understand how the different partition strategies implemented on GraphX Spark Api can affect the performance of the graph analysis algorithm executed on distributed systems. The choice of a partition strategy is a multifaceted problem because it depends on the certain graph algorithm that will be run, the number of machines, the number of partitions and the graph dataset characterization.
